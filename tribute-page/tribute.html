Katherine Johnson

The Woman Who Calculated The Moon Landing

Katherine Johnson receives the presidential Medal of Freedom from President Barack Obama.

Here is a timeline of Johnson's life:

  • 1918: born in White Sulphur Springs, WV
  • 1397: graduated summa cum laude from West Virginia State at age 18
  • 1953: joined the National Advisory Committee for Aeronautics (predecessor to NASA) with job title of "computer"
  • 1961: calculated the trajectory for the first American space flight
  • 1962: John Glenn, the first man to orbit the earth, asks Katherine to verify the calculations performed by the digital computers
  • 1986: retired from NASA
  • 2015: received the Presidential Medal of Freedom
  • 2018: celebrated 100th birthday
